blog.html layout direction

Pick the direction you want me to implement. These are layout-level wireframes based on the current three-column reader: left tree, center article, right recommendations.

A. Tight Reader

Keep the current structure, but fix spacing, sticky sidebars, topbar density, and article width. Lowest risk and best if the current blog interaction is basically right.

B. Workspace Reader

Make the top title area global and reduce the page to a two-column workspace, with recommendations attached to the reader. More modern and less visually crowded.

C. Article First

Give the article most of the viewport and push navigation/recommendations into a narrow right rail. Best for long-form reading, but changes the current mental model the most.

My recommendation

Implement B unless you want a conservative polish pass. It keeps the file tree visible, makes reading calmer, and removes the current three-card feeling without hiding the blog navigation.
